2. Background

This Agreement sets out the terms and conditions under which the Data Processor will process Personal Data on behalf of the Data Controller in compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA).

3. Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement:

  • "POPIA" means the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013
  • "Personal Data" means information relating to an identifiable, living, natural person, and where applicable, an identifiable, existing juristic person
  • "Processing" means any operation or activity concerning Personal Data
  • "Data Subject" means the person to whom Personal Data relates
  • "Information Regulator" means the regulatory body established under POPIA
  • "Information Officer" means the person designated in terms of POPIA Section 55
  • "Responsible Party" means the Data Controller who determines the purpose and means of processing
  • "Operator" means the Data Processor who processes data on behalf of the Responsible Party

5. POPIA Compliance

The Data Processor agrees to comply with all applicable provisions of POPIA and process Personal Data in accordance with the 8 Conditions for Lawful Processing:

  1. Accountability - Taking responsibility for processing activities
  2. Processing Limitation - Processing lawfully and with consent where required
  3. Purpose Specification - Processing only for specified purposes
  4. Further Processing Limitation - Ensuring compatible further processing
  5. Information Quality - Maintaining accurate and complete data
  6. Openness - Being transparent about processing activities
  7. Security Safeguards - Implementing appropriate security measures
  8. Data Subject Participation - Facilitating data subject rights

6. Information Officer Responsibilities

The Data Controller's Information Officer ({{information_officer}}) is responsible for:

  • Encouraging compliance with POPIA conditions
  • Dealing with requests made pursuant to POPIA
  • Working with the Regulator in relation to investigations
  • Ensuring compliance with POPIA provisions

Information Officer Email: {{information_officer_email}}

The Data Processor shall cooperate with the Data Controller's Information Officer in fulfilling these responsibilities.

7. Processor Obligations

The Data Processor agrees to:

  • Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Data Controller
  • Ensure persons authorized to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk
  • Not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization
  • Assist the Data Controller in fulfilling Data Subject rights requests under POPIA
  • Assist the Data Controller in ensuring compliance with security and breach notification obligations
  • At the choice of the Data Controller, delete or return all Personal Data after processing
  • Make available to the Data Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance

8. Security Measures (POPIA Section 19)

The Data Processor shall implement technical and organizational measures as required by POPIA Section 19, including:

  • Identification of reasonably foreseeable risks
  • Establishment of appropriate safeguards against identified risks
  • Regular verification that safeguards are effectively implemented
  • Ensuring safeguards are continually updated
  • Encryption and pseudonymization of Personal Data where appropriate
  • Measures to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, and availability
  • Processes for regular testing and evaluation of security measures
  • Measures to prevent unauthorized access to Personal Data

9. Cross-Border Data Transfers (POPIA Section 72)

Cross-border transfers: {{cross_border}}

The Data Processor shall not transfer Personal Data to a third country unless:

  • The recipient is subject to a law or binding agreement that provides adequate protection
  • The Data Subject has consented to the proposed transfer
  • The transfer is necessary for the performance of a contract
  • The transfer is for the benefit of the Data Subject
  • Other conditions specified in POPIA Section 72 are met

10. Subprocessing

The Data Processor shall not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Data Controller. Where general authorization is given, the Data Processor shall inform the Data Controller of any intended changes and give the Data Controller the opportunity to object.

11. Data Subject Rights

The Data Processor shall assist the Data Controller in responding to Data Subject rights requests under POPIA Sections 23-25, including rights to:

  • Access Personal Data
  • Correction of Personal Data
  • Deletion of Personal Data
  • Object to processing

Privacy Officer Contact: {{privacy_officer}}

12. Personal Data Breaches (POPIA Section 22)

The Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach. The notification must include:

  • Description of the nature of the breach
  • Categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned
  • Categories and approximate number of records concerned
  • Description of measures taken or proposed

The Data Controller must notify the Information Regulator and affected data subjects as required by POPIA Section 22.

13. Audit Rights

The Data Processor shall make available to the Data Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Data Controller or another auditor mandated by the Data Controller.

14. Return or Deletion of Data

At the choice of the Data Controller, the Data Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data to the Data Controller after the end of the provision of services, and delete existing copies unless South African law requires storage of the Personal Data.

15. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of South Africa. Any disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the South African courts.
