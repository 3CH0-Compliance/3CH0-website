POPIA Data Retention Schedule

In terms of Section 14 of the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013

1. Purpose

This Data Retention Schedule establishes the retention periods for personal information processed by {{1.company}} in compliance with Section 14 of POPIA, which requires that personal information must not be retained longer than necessary for achieving the purpose for which it was collected.

2. Legal Framework

Retention periods are determined based on:

  • Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) - Section 14
  • Companies Act 71 of 2008
  • Basic Conditions of Employment Act 75 of 1997 (BCEA)
  • Labour Relations Act 66 of 1995 (LRA)
  • Tax Administration Act 28 of 2011
  • Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002 (ECTA)
  • National Archives and Records Service of South Africa Act 43 of 1996
  • Prescription Act 68 of 1969
  • Industry-specific regulations applicable to {{1.industry}}

Key Principles (Section 14)

  • Records must not be retained longer than necessary for the purpose collected
  • Retention is permitted where required or authorised by law
  • Retention is permitted where reasonably required for lawful purposes
  • Retention is permitted where required by contract
  • Retention is permitted where the data subject has consented
  • After retention period expires, records must be destroyed, deleted, or de-identified

4. Destruction Methods

When retention periods expire, personal information must be destroyed in accordance with Section 14(5) to prevent reconstruction:

4.1 Physical Records

  • Shredding: Cross-cut shredding to DIN 66399 Level P-4 minimum
  • Incineration: Complete burning of documents
  • Pulping: For large volumes of paper records

4.2 Electronic Records

  • Secure deletion: Using certified data destruction software
  • Degaussing: For magnetic media
  • Physical destruction: For storage devices that cannot be securely wiped
  • Cloud data: Verification of deletion from all locations including backups

4.3 De-identification

Where appropriate, personal information may be de-identified rather than destroyed, ensuring:

  • The data subject cannot be identified
  • The information cannot be re-identified by reasonably foreseeable methods
  • The information cannot be linked to other data that identifies the person

Legal Hold Notice

When litigation, regulatory investigation, or audit is reasonably anticipated or pending, a legal hold must be placed on relevant records. During a legal hold:

  • Normal retention schedules are suspended
  • Records must be preserved regardless of retention period
  • The Information Officer must be notified immediately
  • Records may only be destroyed after the legal hold is lifted
