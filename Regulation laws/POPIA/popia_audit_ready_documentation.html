POPIA AUDIT READY DOCUMENTATION

This documentation provides a framework for demonstrating POPIA compliance during regulatory audits, including the mandatory PAIA Manual.

1. Governance Framework

1.1 Information Officer Appointment

Documentation Required:

  • Formal appointment letter for Information Officer
  • Registration with Information Regulator (Section 55)
  • Delegation of authority to Deputy Information Officers

1.2 POPIA Compliance Framework

Documentation Required:

  • Data Protection Policy
  • Privacy Policy
  • Data Retention Policy
  • Information Security Policy
  • Breach Response Plan

2. PAIA Manual

IMPORTANT: The PAIA Manual is a mandatory document under the Promotion of Access to Information Act that every South African company must have. It details how anyone can request information from the organization.

2.1 PAIA Manual Contents

Required by the Promotion of Access to Information Act:

  • Contact details of the Information Officer
  • Guide on how to use the Act (Section 10 guide)
  • Description of the organization's structure and functions
  • Categories of records available without a formal request
  • Description of subjects on which records are held
  • Request procedures and fees
  • Remedies available when access is refused
  • Description of services available to the public

2.2 PAIA Manual Maintenance

The PAIA Manual must be:

  • Updated regularly (at least annually)
  • Made available on the organization's website
  • Available for inspection at the organization's offices
  • Submitted to the Information Regulator upon request

3. Processing Documentation

3.1 Processing Activities Register

Required by POPIA Section 17

Maintain a record of processing activities containing:

  • Name and contact details of responsible party
  • Purpose of processing
  • Description of data subjects and categories of personal information
  • Categories of recipients
  • Transfers to third countries
  • Time limits for erasure
  • Description of security measures

3.2 Lawful Basis Documentation

For each processing activity, document the lawful basis under POPIA Section 11:

  • Consent records
  • Contractual necessity documentation
  • Legal obligation references
  • Legitimate interest assessments

4. Data Subject Rights Management

4.1 Rights Procedure Documentation

Required by POPIA Sections 23-25

Documented procedures for handling:

  • Access requests
  • Correction requests
  • Deletion requests
  • Objections to processing

4.2 Request Log

Maintain a log of all data subject requests containing:

  • Date of request
  • Nature of request
  • Date of response
  • Outcome

5. Information Security

5.1 Security Measures Documentation

Required by POPIA Section 19

Documentation of:

  • Risk assessment procedures
  • Technical security measures (encryption, access controls)
  • Organizational security measures (policies, training)
  • Regular testing and evaluation of measures

5.2 Access Control Documentation

Documentation of:

  • User access management procedures
  • Access rights reviews
  • Privileged access management

6. Third-Party Management

6.1 Processor Agreements

Required by POPIA Section 21

Written contracts with all processors containing:

  • Subject matter and duration of processing
  • Nature and purpose of processing
  • Type of personal data and categories of data subjects
  • Obligations and rights of the controller
  • Security requirements

6.2 Due Diligence Records

Documentation of due diligence conducted on processors.

7. Data Breach Management

7.1 Breach Response Plan

Required by POPIA Section 22

Documented procedures for:

  • Identifying and containing breaches
  • Assessing risk to data subjects
  • Notifying the Information Regulator
  • Notifying data subjects when required
  • Documenting the breach response

7.2 Breach Register

Record of all personal data breaches containing:

  • Date and time of breach
  • Nature of breach
  • Categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned
  • Measures taken to address the breach

8. Training and Awareness

8.1 Training Records

Documentation of:

  • POPIA training programs
  • Attendance records
  • Training materials
  • Assessment results

9. Data Protection Impact Assessments

9.1 DPIA Procedure

Documented procedure for conducting DPIAs for high-risk processing.

9.2 DPIA Records

Records of DPIAs conducted, including:

  • Description of processing operations
  • Assessment of necessity and proportionality
  • Risk assessment
  • Measures to address risks

10. Direct Marketing Compliance

10.1 Marketing Consent Records

Required by POPIA Section 69

Documentation of:

  • Consent obtained for direct marketing
  • Opt-out mechanisms
  • Procedures for honoring objections

11. International Transfers

11.1 Transfer Documentation

Required by POPIA Section 72

For transfers outside South Africa, documentation of:

  • Adequacy decisions
  • Appropriate safeguards
  • Derogations for specific situations

12. Ongoing Compliance Monitoring

12.1 Internal Audit Program

Documented program for regular POPIA compliance audits.

12.2 Compliance Review Schedule

Schedule for regular review of policies and procedures.

13. Evidence of Compliance

13.1 Compliance Checklists

Completed checklists demonstrating adherence to POPIA requirements.

13.2 Management Review Records

Minutes of meetings where POPIA compliance was reviewed.

13.3 Continuous Improvement Records

Documentation of actions taken to address compliance gaps.
