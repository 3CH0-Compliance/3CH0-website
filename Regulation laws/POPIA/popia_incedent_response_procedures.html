1. Purpose and Scope

This procedure establishes the framework for responding to security compromises involving personal information in compliance with Section 22 of POPIA. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who process personal information on behalf of {{1.company}}.

What is a Security Compromise?

A security compromise occurs when there are reasonable grounds to believe that personal information has been accessed or acquired by any unauthorised person. This includes:

  • Unauthorized access to systems containing personal information
  • Lost or stolen devices (laptops, phones, USB drives) containing personal data
  • Accidental disclosure (emails sent to wrong recipients)
  • Malware, ransomware, or phishing attacks
  • Physical break-ins where data may have been accessed
  • Insider threats or unauthorized employee access
  • Third-party/vendor breaches affecting our data

3. Incident Response Phases

PHASE 1: DETECTION AND REPORTING

Objective: Identify and report the incident immediately

Anyone who discovers or suspects an incident must:

  1. STOP - Do not attempt to investigate or fix the issue yourself
  2. PRESERVE - Do not delete, modify, or turn off affected systems (unless necessary to prevent further damage)
  3. REPORT - Immediately notify:
    • Your direct supervisor
    • Information Officer: {{1.information_officer}}
    • IT Security: {{1.it_security_contact}}
  4. DOCUMENT - Record:
    • Date and time of discovery
    • How the incident was discovered
    • Type of information potentially affected
    • Systems or locations involved
    • Any actions already taken

PHASE 2: CONTAINMENT

Objective: Limit the impact and prevent further unauthorized access

Immediate Containment Actions:

Isolate affected systems from the network
Disable compromised user accounts
Change passwords for affected systems
Block suspicious IP addresses
Preserve evidence (logs, screenshots, affected files)
Activate backup systems if necessary
Notify relevant third parties/operators

Responsible: IT Security Team in coordination with Information Officer

PHASE 3: ASSESSMENT

Objective: Determine the scope and impact of the breach

Assessment Checklist:

What type of personal information was affected?
Was special personal information involved?
Was children's information involved?
How many data subjects are affected?
Who had unauthorized access?
How long did the breach last?
What is the likelihood of harm to data subjects?
What is the nature of potential harm?
Is the breach ongoing?
Are there regulatory implications?

Responsible: Information Officer with IT Security and Legal

PHASE 4: NOTIFICATION (Section 22)

Objective: Notify the Information Regulator and affected data subjects

‚è∞ Notification Timeline

Notification must be made as soon as reasonably possible after discovery, taking into account:

  • Legitimate needs of law enforcement
  • Measures needed to determine the scope of the compromise
  • Measures needed to restore system integrity

4.1 Information Regulator Notification

Contact: complaints.IR@justice.gov.za

The notification must include:

  • Description of the nature of the security compromise
  • Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
  • Categories and approximate number of personal information records affected
  • Name and contact details of Information Officer
  • Likely consequences of the security compromise
  • Measures taken or proposed to address the compromise

4.2 Data Subject Notification

Notification methods (Section 22(4)):

  • Mail to last known physical or postal address
  • Email to last known email address
  • Prominent notice on company website
  • Publication in news media
  • As directed by the Information Regulator

The notification must provide:

  • Description of the possible consequences
  • Measures the company intends to take or has taken
  • Recommendations for protective measures the data subject can take
  • Identity of the unauthorized person (if known)
Note: Notification to data subjects may be delayed if a public body responsible for prevention, detection, or investigation of offenses determines it would impede a criminal investigation.

PHASE 5: ERADICATION AND RECOVERY

Objective: Remove the threat and restore normal operations

Eradication Actions:

Remove malware/unauthorized access points
Patch vulnerabilities that were exploited
Reset all potentially compromised credentials
Review and update access controls
Implement additional security measures

Recovery Actions:

Restore systems from clean backups
Verify system integrity before returning to production
Monitor systems for signs of continued compromise
Gradually restore services
Document all recovery actions

PHASE 6: POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

Objective: Learn from the incident and improve defenses

Post-Incident Review Meeting:

To be held within 14 days of incident closure

Review Agenda:

  1. Timeline of events
  2. Effectiveness of response
  3. Root cause analysis
  4. What worked well
  5. Areas for improvement
  6. Required policy/procedure updates
  7. Training needs identified
  8. Security improvements required

Documentation:

  • Complete incident report
  • Lessons learned document
  • Updated risk assessment
  • Remediation action plan
