HIPAA Privacy Policy Template

1. Purpose and Scope

This Privacy Policy establishes how [Organization Name] collects, uses, maintains, and discloses protected health information (PHI) in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Privacy Rule.

This policy applies to all workforce members, including employees, volunteers, trainees, and other persons whose conduct is under the direct control of [Organization Name].

2. Definitions

Protected Health Information (PHI): Individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium.

Covered Entity: Health plans, health care clearinghouses, and health care providers who transmit health information electronically.

Business Associate: A person or entity that performs certain functions or activities on behalf of a covered entity involving the use or disclosure of PHI.

3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

[Organization Name] may use or disclose PHI without individual authorization for:

  • Treatment, payment, and health care operations
  • When required by law
  • Public health activities
  • Victims of abuse, neglect, or domestic violence
  • Health oversight activities
  • Judicial and administrative proceedings
  • Law enforcement purposes
  • Decedents
  • Cadaveric organ, eye, or tissue donation
  • Research
  • Serious threat to health or safety
  • Essential government functions
  • Workers' compensation

4. Minimum Necessary Standard

[Organization Name] will make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, and request only the minimum necessary PHI to accomplish the intended purpose, except for:

  • Disclosures to or requests by a health care provider for treatment
  • Uses or disclosures made pursuant to an authorization
  • Disclosures to the individual
  • Uses or disclosures required by law
  • Uses or disclosures required for compliance with HIPAA

5. Individual Rights

Individuals have the right to:

  • Receive a notice of privacy practices
  • Access and obtain a copy of their PHI
  • Request amendments to their PHI
  • Receive an accounting of disclosures
  • Request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures
  • Request confidential communications
  • File a complaint about privacy practices

6. Administrative Requirements

[Organization Name] will:

  • Designate a privacy official responsible for developing and implementing privacy policies
  • Train all workforce members on privacy policies and procedures
  • Implement appropriate sanctions against workforce members who violate policies
  • Mitigate harmful effects of known violations
  • Maintain reasonable and appropriate safeguards to protect PHI
  • Retain required documentation for six years

7. Complaints

Individuals may file complaints with [Organization Name] by contacting [Privacy Official Name] at [Contact Information]. Individuals may also file a complaint with the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

8. Policy Review and Updates

This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure ongoing compliance with HIPAA regulations.

This template should be customized to reflect your organization's specific practices and procedures. Consult with legal counsel to ensure full compliance with applicable laws.
