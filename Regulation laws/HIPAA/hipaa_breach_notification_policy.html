1. Purpose

This policy establishes procedures for identifying, investigating, and responding to breaches of unsecured Protected Health Information (PHI) in compliance with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule. {{1.company}} is committed to protecting the privacy and security of all PHI and to promptly notifying affected individuals, the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS), and when required, the media, in the event of a breach.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all workforce members of {{1.company}}, including employees, volunteers, trainees, contractors, and any other persons whose conduct is under the direct control of {{1.company}}. This policy also applies to all Business Associates through their Business Associate Agreements.

3. Definitions

3.1 Breach

The acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

Presumption of Breach: An impermissible use or disclosure of PHI is presumed to be a breach unless {{1.company}} demonstrates that there is a low probability that the PHI has been compromised based on a risk assessment.

3.2 Unsecured PHI

PHI that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized persons through:

  • Encryption: Using an algorithmic process that meets NIST standards
  • Destruction: Paper records shredded or destroyed; electronic media cleared, purged, or destroyed per NIST guidelines

3.3 Compromise

The PHI is compromised when its security or privacy has been placed at risk.

4. Breach Risk Assessment

Upon discovery of an impermissible use or disclosure, {{1.company}} shall conduct a risk assessment considering at least the following factors:

Factor Assessment Questions
1. Nature and Extent of PHI What types of identifiers were involved? What is the likelihood of re-identification? Does it include sensitive information (SSN, financial, clinical)?
2. Unauthorized Person Who received or accessed the PHI? Are they obligated to protect it (e.g., another covered entity)?
3. Actual Acquisition/Viewing Was the PHI actually acquired or viewed? Or was there only an opportunity for access?
4. Extent of Mitigation What steps were taken to mitigate risk? Was the PHI returned or destroyed? Were assurances obtained?

Documentation Required: All risk assessments must be documented, including the factors considered, the determination reached, and the rationale. This documentation must be retained for six (6) years.

5. Exceptions to Breach Definition

The following do NOT constitute a breach:

  1. Unintentional Acquisition: Made in good faith, within the scope of authority, and not further impermissibly used or disclosed
  2. Inadvertent Disclosure: Between authorized persons at the same covered entity or business associate, and not further impermissibly used or disclosed
  3. Good Faith Belief: The unauthorized person to whom the disclosure was made would not reasonably have been able to retain the information

7. Notification Requirements and Timelines

Critical Timelines

Notification Type Deadline Method
Individual Notification Without unreasonable delay, no later than 60 days from discovery First-class mail (or email if agreed)
HHS Notification (500+ individuals) No later than 60 days from discovery HHS Breach Portal
HHS Notification (<500 individuals) Within 60 days of end of calendar year in which breach discovered Annual log via HHS Breach Portal
Media Notification No later than 60 days (when 500+ residents of a state/jurisdiction affected) Prominent media outlets in the state
Business Associate to Covered Entity Without unreasonable delay, no later than 30 days from discovery As specified in BAA

Discovery Date: A breach is considered "discovered" on the first day it is known or should reasonably have been known by any workforce member (not just management). This starts the notification clock.

8. Content of Individual Notification

The notification to individuals must include:

  1. Brief description of what happened, including date of breach and date of discovery
  2. Types of PHI involved (e.g., name, SSN, diagnosis, treatment information)
  3. Steps individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm
  4. Brief description of what {{1.company}} is doing to investigate, mitigate harm, and prevent future breaches
  5. Contact information including a toll-free number, email address, website, or postal address for questions

Substitute Notice: If contact information is insufficient or outdated for 10 or more individuals, substitute notice must be provided via website posting (at least 90 days) and major media outlets.

9. HHS Notification

9.1 Breaches Affecting 500 or More Individuals

Notify HHS at the same time individuals are notified via the HHS Breach Portal at: https://ocrportal.hhs.gov/ocr/breach/wizard_breach.jsf

9.2 Breaches Affecting Fewer Than 500 Individuals

Maintain a log of all breaches and submit annually to HHS within 60 days of the end of the calendar year.

10. Law Enforcement Delay

Notification may be delayed if a law enforcement official provides a written statement that notification would impede a criminal investigation or cause damage to national security. Oral requests can delay notification for up to 30 days (must be documented).

11. Documentation and Retention

{{1.company}} shall document and retain for six (6) years:

  • All breach risk assessments
  • Investigation reports
  • Determinations and rationale
  • Copies of all notifications sent
  • Business associate breach reports
  • Corrective action plans
  • Evidence of staff training related to the breach

13. Training

All workforce members shall receive training on this policy upon hire and annually thereafter. Training shall include:

  • Definition of a breach
  • Examples of potential breaches
  • How to recognize a breach
  • Reporting procedures and contacts
  • Consequences of failing to report

14. Sanctions

Workforce members who fail to report a suspected breach, delay reporting, or obstruct an investigation may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination, in accordance with {{1.company}}'s Sanction Policy.

15. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in law, regulations, or organizational practices.
