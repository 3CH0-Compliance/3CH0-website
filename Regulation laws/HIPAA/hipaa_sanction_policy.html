1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for applying appropriate sanctions against workforce members who violate {{1.company}}'s HIPAA policies and procedures or the HIPAA Privacy, Security, or Breach Notification Rules. The purpose of this policy is to:

  • Ensure compliance with HIPAA regulations
  • Protect the privacy and security of Protected Health Information (PHI)
  • Deter violations through consistent and fair enforcement
  • Provide a clear framework for addressing violations

2. Scope

This policy applies to all workforce members of {{1.company}}, including but not limited to:

  • Full-time and part-time employees
  • Temporary employees
  • Volunteers
  • Trainees and students
  • Contractors working on-site
  • Any other persons whose conduct is under the direct control of {{1.company}}

3. Policy Statement

{{1.company}} is committed to maintaining the highest standards of privacy and security for PHI. All workforce members are expected to comply with HIPAA regulations and {{1.company}}'s privacy and security policies. Violations will result in sanctions appropriate to the nature and severity of the violation.

No Retaliation: {{1.company}} prohibits retaliation against any workforce member who in good faith reports a HIPAA violation or suspected violation, files a complaint with HHS, or participates in an investigation. Retaliation itself is a violation subject to sanctions.

4. Violation Categories and Examples

Level 1: Minor Violations (Unintentional/First-Time)

Characteristics: Unintentional, no harm occurred, first offense, minimal risk to PHI
Examples Typical Sanction
  • Forgetting to log off a workstation (no PHI accessed by unauthorized person)
  • Discussing PHI in semi-public area (not overheard)
  • Minor documentation errors
  • Unintentional failure to secure paper records
  • Verbal warning
  • Re-training
  • Documented counseling

Level 2: Moderate Violations (Negligent/Repeated Minor)

Characteristics: Negligent behavior, pattern of minor violations, moderate risk to PHI, potential for harm
Examples Typical Sanction
  • Repeated failure to log off workstations
  • Sharing passwords with coworkers
  • Improper disposal of PHI (without actual breach)
  • Failure to use encryption when required
  • Discussing PHI inappropriately where overheard
  • Failure to complete required HIPAA training
  • Written warning
  • Mandatory re-training
  • Performance improvement plan
  • Temporary loss of system access

Level 3: Serious Violations (Reckless/Harmful)

Characteristics: Reckless disregard for policies, actual or significant risk of harm, substantial breach of trust
Examples Typical Sanction
  • Accessing PHI without authorization (curiosity/no disclosure)
  • Sharing PHI with unauthorized persons (unintentional)
  • Failure to report a known breach
  • Circumventing security controls
  • Installing unauthorized software that compromises security
  • Loss of unencrypted device containing PHI
  • Final written warning
  • Suspension without pay
  • Demotion
  • Transfer to non-PHI role
  • Revocation of access privileges

Level 4: Severe/Willful Violations

Characteristics: Intentional, malicious, or for personal gain; significant harm to individuals or organization; criminal conduct
Examples Typical Sanction
  • Intentionally accessing/disclosing PHI without authorization
  • Selling or using PHI for personal gain
  • Identity theft or fraud using PHI
  • Deliberately destroying or altering PHI
  • Intentionally circumventing security to access PHI
  • Accessing PHI of celebrities, family, or acquaintances without authorization
  • Retaliation against someone who reported a violation
  • Immediate termination
  • Referral to law enforcement
  • Civil litigation as appropriate

6. Sanction Procedures

6.1 Reporting Violations

All suspected violations should be reported immediately to:

  • Privacy Officer: {{1.privacy_officer}}
  • Security Officer: {{1.security_officer}}
  • Email: {{1.email}}

6.2 Investigation

Upon receiving a report of a violation:

  1. The Privacy Officer and/or Security Officer will conduct an investigation
  2. The workforce member will be notified and given an opportunity to respond
  3. Evidence will be gathered and documented
  4. Findings will be documented in writing

6.3 Determination

The Privacy Officer, Security Officer, and appropriate management will:

  1. Review the investigation findings
  2. Consider the factors listed in Section 5
  3. Determine the appropriate sanction
  4. Document the decision and rationale

6.4 Implementation

  1. The workforce member will be notified of the sanction in writing
  2. The sanction will be implemented by Human Resources and/or management
  3. Documentation will be placed in the workforce member's personnel file
  4. The sanction will be logged in the HIPAA Sanction Log

6.5 Appeal

Workforce members may appeal sanctions through {{1.company}}'s standard grievance or appeal process as outlined in the Employee Handbook.

7. Documentation Requirements

All sanctions must be documented and retained for a minimum of six (6) years. Documentation shall include:

  • Date of violation
  • Description of the violation
  • Investigation findings
  • Factors considered
  • Sanction imposed
  • Date of sanction
  • Notification to workforce member
  • Any corrective action or training required

9. Criminal Referral

{{1.company}} will refer matters to law enforcement when violations involve:

  • Theft or sale of PHI
  • Identity theft or fraud
  • Other criminal conduct

Workforce members should be aware that HIPAA violations can result in criminal penalties of up to $250,000 in fines and 10 years imprisonment.

10. Training and Awareness

All workforce members will be trained on this Sanction Policy during initial HIPAA training and annual refresher training. Training will include:

  • Overview of the Sanction Policy
  • Examples of violations and consequences
  • Reporting procedures
  • Anti-retaliation protections
