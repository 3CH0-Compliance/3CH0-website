RECITALS

WHEREAS, Business and Service Provider/Contractor have entered into an agreement under which Service Provider/Contractor will provide certain services to Business (the "Services Agreement");

WHEREAS, in connection with the Services Agreement, Service Provider/Contractor may process Personal Information of California consumers on behalf of Business;

WHEREAS, the parties wish to ensure that such processing complies with the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020 (collectively, "CCPA");

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS

Terms used but not defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings set forth in the CCPA (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140). Additionally:

  1. "Business" means the entity identified above that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Information.
  2. "Business Purpose" means the use of Personal Information for the Business's operational purposes, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(e).
  3. "Consumer" means a natural person who is a California resident.
  4. "Contractor" means a person to whom the Business makes available Personal Information for a Business Purpose pursuant to a written contract, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(j).
  5. "Personal Information" means information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked with a particular Consumer or household, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(v).
  6. "Sensitive Personal Information" means Personal Information revealing a Consumer's social security number, driver's license, financial account information, precise geolocation, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, union membership, genetic data, neural data, biometric information, health information, or sex life/sexual orientation, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ae).
  7. "Service Provider" means a person that processes Personal Information on behalf of a Business pursuant to a written contract, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ag).
  8. "Sell" means selling, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating Personal Information to a third party for monetary or other valuable consideration, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ad).
  9. "Share" means sharing, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating Personal Information for cross-context behavioral advertising, as defined in Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ah).

3. SERVICE PROVIDER/CONTRACTOR OBLIGATIONS

Service Provider/Contractor certifies that it understands and will comply with the following restrictions (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(ag) and (j)):

3.1 Prohibition on Selling and Sharing

Service Provider/Contractor shall NOT:

  • Sell the Personal Information received from Business
  • Share the Personal Information for cross-context behavioral advertising

3.2 Use Restrictions

Service Provider/Contractor shall NOT retain, use, or disclose Personal Information:

  • For any purpose other than the Business Purposes specified in Section 2.3
  • For any commercial purpose other than the Business Purposes specified in this Agreement
  • Outside of the direct business relationship between Service Provider/Contractor and Business

3.3 Prohibition on Combining Data

Service Provider/Contractor shall NOT combine the Personal Information received from Business with:

  • Personal Information received from or on behalf of another person or persons
  • Personal Information collected from its own interaction with consumers

Exception: Combination is permitted to perform Business Purposes as defined in CCPA regulations, excluding cross-context behavioral advertising for opted-out consumers.

3.4 Sensitive Personal Information

When processing Sensitive Personal Information, Service Provider/Contractor shall:

  • Limit use to that which is necessary to perform the services reasonably expected
  • Comply with any consumer directions to limit use received from Business
  • Not use Sensitive Personal Information after receiving instructions from Business that the consumer has exercised their right to limit

3.5 Certification

CERTIFICATION (Required by Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(j)(1)(B) and (ag)(1)(D)):

Service Provider/Contractor certifies that it understands and will comply with all restrictions set forth in Section 3 of this Agreement and applicable provisions of the CCPA.

4. COMPLIANCE MONITORING AND AUDITS

4.1 Monitoring Rights (Required by Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(j)(1)(C))

Business may monitor Service Provider/Contractor's compliance with this Agreement through:

  • Ongoing manual reviews
  • Automated scans
  • Regular assessments, audits, or other technical and operational testing

4.2 Audit Frequency

At minimum, compliance audits shall be conducted at least once every twelve (12) months.

4.3 Audit Cooperation

Service Provider/Contractor shall:

  • Provide reasonable access to facilities, systems, and records
  • Make relevant personnel available for interviews
  • Provide documentation of compliance measures
  • Remediate any identified deficiencies within [30/60/90] days

  • All the same restrictions and obligations set forth in Section 3
  • Provide the same level of privacy protection required by this Agreement
  • Comply with applicable CCPA requirements

6. CONSUMER RIGHTS ASSISTANCE

6.1 Assistance Obligation

Service Provider/Contractor shall assist Business in responding to verifiable Consumer requests, including:

  • Right to Know: Provide Consumer's Personal Information in Service Provider/Contractor's possession
  • Right to Delete: Delete Consumer's Personal Information upon Business's direction
  • Right to Correct: Correct inaccurate Personal Information or enable Business to do so
  • Right to Opt-Out: Comply with opt-out directions communicated by Business
  • Right to Limit: Limit use of Sensitive Personal Information as directed

6.2 Response Timeline

Service Provider/Contractor shall respond to Business's requests for assistance within [10/15] business days to enable Business to meet the 45-day response deadline.

6.3 Direct Consumer Requests

Service Provider/Contractor is not required to comply with verifiable Consumer requests received directly from Consumers for Personal Information collected in its role as a Service Provider/Contractor. Such requests shall be forwarded to Business within [5] business days.

7. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

7.1 Reasonable Security Measures

Service Provider/Contractor shall implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices appropriate to the nature of the Personal Information, including:

  • Administrative safeguards: Security policies, employee training, access controls, incident response procedures
  • Technical safeguards: Encryption, firewalls, intrusion detection, secure authentication
  • Physical safeguards: Facility access controls, secure disposal, device security

7.2 Encryption

Personal Information shall be encrypted:

  • In transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • At rest using AES-256 or equivalent

8. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

8.1 Notification Timeline

Service Provider/Contractor shall notify Business of any actual or suspected data breach affecting Personal Information without unreasonable delay and in no event later than [24/48/72] hours after discovery.

8.2 Notification Contents

Breach notification shall include:

  • Description of the nature of the breach
  • Categories and approximate number of Consumers affected
  • Categories of Personal Information involved
  • Contact information for further inquiries
  • Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

8.3 Cooperation

Service Provider/Contractor shall cooperate with Business in investigating and remediating the breach, including providing all information necessary for Business to comply with notification requirements under Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.82.

9. COMPLIANCE NOTIFICATIONS

9.1 Inability to Comply (Required by Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100(d)(4))

Service Provider/Contractor shall notify Business if it makes a determination that it can no longer meet its obligations under the CCPA.

9.2 Business's Remediation Rights (Required by Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100(d)(5))

Upon receiving notice under Section 9.1, or upon independent determination of non-compliance, Business shall have the right to take reasonable and appropriate steps to:

  • Stop unauthorized use of Personal Information
  • Remediate any unauthorized use
  • Terminate this Agreement and the Services Agreement

10. TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL MEASURES

Service Provider/Contractor shall assist Business in complying with its obligations under Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.100(d)-(f), taking into account the nature of the processing, including obligations related to:

  • Data minimization
  • Purpose limitation
  • Security measures
  • Consumer rights fulfillment

11. DATA RETURN AND DELETION

11.1 Upon Termination

Upon termination of this Agreement or the Services Agreement, Service Provider/Contractor shall, at Business's direction:

  • Return all Personal Information to Business in a structured, commonly used format; and/or
  • Delete all Personal Information, including all copies, backups, and archives

11.2 Certification of Deletion

Upon completion of deletion, Service Provider/Contractor shall provide written certification that all Personal Information has been deleted.

11.3 Retention Exceptions

Service Provider/Contractor may retain Personal Information only to the extent required by applicable law, in which case it shall:

  • Notify Business of the legal requirement
  • Continue to protect such information in accordance with this Agreement
  • Delete the information when the legal obligation ceases

12. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

12.1 Service Provider/Contractor Liability

Service Provider/Contractor shall be liable for its own violations of the CCPA and this Agreement.

12.2 Business Liability Protection

Business shall not be liable under the CCPA if Service Provider/Contractor uses Personal Information in violation of this Agreement, provided that Business did not have actual knowledge or reason to believe that Service Provider/Contractor intended to commit such violation at the time of disclosure.

12.3 Indemnification

Service Provider/Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Business from any claims, damages, fines, or penalties arising from Service Provider/Contractor's violation of this Agreement or the CCPA.

13. TERM AND TERMINATION

13.1 Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for the duration of the Services Agreement.

13.2 Termination for Breach

Business may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Service Provider/Contractor breaches any material provision of this Agreement.

13.3 Survival

Sections 3 (Obligations), 7 (Security), 8 (Breach Notification), 11 (Data Return/Deletion), and 12 (Liability) shall survive termination.

14. GENERAL PROVISIONS

14.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

14.2 Conflicts

In the event of conflict between this Agreement and the Services Agreement, this Agreement shall prevail with respect to data protection matters.

14.3 Amendments

This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties. Business may update this Agreement to reflect changes to the CCPA upon reasonable notice to Service Provider/Contractor.

14.4 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding CCPA compliance and supersedes all prior agreements on this subject.
