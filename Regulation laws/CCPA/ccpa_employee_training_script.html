1. What is CCPA?

CCPA is the California Consumer Privacy Act, which:

  • Took effect January 1, 2020
  • Gives California residents control over their personal information
  • Applies to businesses meeting certain thresholds
  • Was enhanced by CPRA (California Privacy Rights Act) in 2023

Penalties: Up to $7,500 per intentional violation!

4. Consumer Rights Under CCPA

Right to Know

Consumers can request:

  • What personal information we collected
  • Where we got it from
  • Why we collected it
  • Who we shared it with

Right to Delete

Consumers can request we delete their personal information (with some exceptions).

Right to Opt-Out

Consumers can say "Do Not Sell My Personal Information."

Do we sell data? {{1.sell_data}}

Right to Non-Discrimination

We cannot treat consumers differently for exercising their rights.

Right to Correct

Consumers can request correction of inaccurate information.

5. Handling Consumer Requests

When a Consumer Makes a Request:

  1. DO NOT ignore or dismiss the request
  2. Immediately forward to {{1.privacy_officer}}
  3. Document the date and nature of the request
  4. Do not share any data until identity is verified

Timeline: We have 45 days to respond (can extend by 45 more if needed).

7. Practical Scenarios

Scenario 1: Phone Request

Action: Thank them, take their contact info, and immediately forward to {{1.privacy_officer}}. Do NOT share any data over the phone.

Scenario 2: Email Request

Situation: You receive an email: "Please delete all my information immediately."

Action: Forward the email to {{1.email}} and {{1.privacy_officer}} right away. Do not reply yourself.

Scenario 3: Opt-Out Request

Situation: A customer clicks "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" on our website.

Action: This should be handled automatically, but report any issues to {{1.privacy_officer}}.

8. Do's and Don'ts

DO:

  • Forward all privacy requests to {{1.privacy_officer}}
  • Document when requests come in

DON'T:

  • Share data without verification
  • Try to handle requests on your own
  • Treat customers differently for exercising rights
