What is CCPA?

  • California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018
  • Enhanced by California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) in 2023
  • Gives California residents control over their personal information
  • Applies to businesses meeting certain thresholds

Why Does This Matter?

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  • $2,500 per unintentional violation
  • $7,500 per intentional violation
  • $7,500 per violation involving a minor's data

These penalties apply PER VIOLATION - they add up quickly!

MODULE 4: CONSUMER RIGHTS UNDER CCPA

1. Right to Know

Consumers can request:

  • What personal information we collected
  • Where we got it from
  • Why we collected it
  • Who we shared it with
  • The specific pieces of information we have

2. Right to Delete

Consumers can request we delete their personal information (with some exceptions for legal compliance, ongoing transactions, security, etc.)

3. Right to Correct

Consumers can request correction of inaccurate personal information.

4. Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing

Consumers can say "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information."

Does [COMPANY NAME] sell data? [YES/NO]

5. Right to Limit Sensitive Personal Information (NEW)

Consumers can limit how we use their sensitive personal information to only what's necessary for the services they requested.

6. Right to Non-Discrimination

We CANNOT treat consumers differently for exercising their rights - no higher prices, worse service, or denial of service.

MODULE 5: HANDLING CONSUMER REQUESTS

THE GOLDEN RULE: FORWARD IMMEDIATELY

When you receive ANY privacy request:

  1. DO NOT ignore or dismiss the request
  2. DO NOT try to handle it yourself
  3. DO NOT share any data without verification
  4. IMMEDIATELY forward to [PRIVACY OFFICER]
  5. Document the date and nature of the request

Response Timeline

  • 45 days to respond to requests
  • Can extend by another 45 days if needed (must notify consumer)
  • This is why you must forward requests IMMEDIATELY

Opt-Out Preference Signals (Global Privacy Control)

Some browsers send automatic "do not sell" signals. We are required to honor these signals.

MODULE 6: PROTECTING MINORS

SPECIAL RULES FOR MINORS:
  • Under 13: Need PARENT/GUARDIAN consent to sell/share data
  • Ages 13-15: Need the MINOR'S OWN opt-in consent
  • Penalty: $7,500 per violation involving a minor's data

If you suspect a user is a minor, alert [PRIVACY OFFICER] immediately!

MODULE 7: PRACTICAL SCENARIOS

SCENARIO 1: Phone Request

Situation: A caller says "I'm a California resident and I want to know what data you have on me."

CORRECT ACTION:

  1. Thank them for calling
  2. Collect their contact information
  3. Let them know our Privacy Officer will follow up
  4. Immediately forward to [PRIVACY OFFICER]
  5. DO NOT share any data over the phone

SCENARIO 2: Email Delete Request

Situation: You receive an email: "Please delete all my information immediately."

CORRECT ACTION:

  1. Forward the email to [PRIVACY EMAIL]
  2. CC [PRIVACY OFFICER]
  3. DO NOT reply yourself or delete anything

SCENARIO 3: Request to Limit Sensitive Data

Situation: Customer says "I don't want you using my location data for anything except what I need."

CORRECT ACTION:

  1. Acknowledge their request
  2. Forward to [PRIVACY OFFICER] - this is a "Right to Limit" request
  3. Document the date and details

SCENARIO 4: Suspicious Request (Possible Minor)

Situation: A user mentions they're "in high school" or otherwise indicates they might be under 16.

CORRECT ACTION:

  1. Flag this account/interaction immediately
  2. Notify [PRIVACY OFFICER] with all details
  3. DO NOT sell or share this person's data

MODULE 8: DO'S AND DON'TS

DO: DON'T:
  • Forward ALL privacy requests to Privacy Officer
  • Document when requests come in
  • Protect personal information at all times
  • Ask if unsure about ANYTHING
  • Treat all consumers equally regardless of rights exercised
  • Report suspected data breaches immediately
  • Ignore consumer requests
  • Share data without verification
  • Try to handle requests on your own
  • Treat customers differently for exercising rights
  • Guess or assume - always verify
  • Sell/share minor's data without proper consent
