Introduction to GDPR

Trainer Script: "Welcome to our GDPR training session. The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that came into effect on May 25, 2018. It applies to all organizations processing personal data of individuals in the EU, regardless of where the organization is located."

Key Points to Cover:

  • GDPR protects personal data of individuals in the EU
  • Applies to all companies processing EU residents' data
  • Significant fines for non-compliance (up to 4% of global turnover or â‚¬20 million)
  • Gives individuals more control over their personal data

Key GDPR Principles

Trainer Script: "Let's review the core principles that guide how we handle personal data at [Company Name]:"

  • Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: We process personal data legally, fairly, and transparently
  • Purpose limitation: We only collect data for specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes
  • Data minimization: We only collect data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary
  • Accuracy: We keep personal data accurate and up to date
  • Storage limitation: We keep personal data in identifiable form only as long as necessary
  • Integrity and confidentiality: We process personal data securely
  • Accountability: We are responsible for and must demonstrate compliance

  • Identification numbers

Recognizing and Handling Data Subject Requests

Trainer Script: "Individuals have rights regarding their personal data. If someone contacts you asking about their data rights, you should:"

  1. Listen carefully to their request
  2. Do not attempt to handle the request yourself
  3. Immediately direct them to [Data Protection Officer/Privacy Team Contact]
  4. Document the date and time of the request
  5. Be polite and professional

Individual Rights Under GDPR:

  • Right to be informed
  • Right of access
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure (right to be forgotten)
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object
  • Rights related to automated decision making and profiling

Data Security Best Practices

Trainer Script: "Follow these security practices to protect personal data at [Company Name]:"

  • Use strong, unique passwords and change them regularly
  • Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • Be cautious of phishing emails - don't click on suspicious links
  • Encrypt sensitive data when storing or transmitting
  • Securely dispose of paper documents containing personal data
  • Use secure Wi-Fi networks, especially when working remotely
  • Follow our [Company-specific security policy]

Scenario-Based Training

Scenario 1: Customer Data Request

Situation: A customer emails asking for a copy of all the data you have about them.

Question: What should you do?

Correct Response: Forward the request immediately to [Data Protection Officer/Privacy Team]. Do not attempt to gather or send any data yourself.

Scenario 2: Suspicious Phone Call

Situation: You receive a phone call from someone claiming to be from IT, asking for your password to fix a system issue.

Question: What should you do?

Correct Response: Never share your password. Legitimate IT staff will never ask for your password. Report this incident to your manager and the IT department.

Scenario 3: Lost Device

Situation: You realize you've left your company laptop on public transportation.

Question: What are the immediate steps you should take?

Correct Response: Immediately contact [IT Security/Manager] to report the loss and follow company procedures for lost devices.

Data Breach Response

Trainer Script: "If you suspect a data breach has occurred, you must:"

  1. Immediately report it to [Data Protection Officer/Security Team]
  2. Do not attempt to investigate or resolve it yourself
  3. Document what you observed
  4. Follow company procedures for incident response

What constitutes a data breach?

  • Loss or theft of devices containing personal data
  • Unauthorized access to systems
  • Sending personal data to the wrong recipient
  • Alteration or destruction of personal data without authorization
